About Dan

I am currently a 1st year Ph.D. candidate studying under Dr. Tingjian Ge at the University of Massachusetts, Lowell. Previously, I recevied my Master's of Science at UML. I am a part of the Data Mining and Database research group where we study how to apply data mining and machine learning techniques on large databases in an efficient manner. We have also done research on the use of machine learning techniques to optimize data that is structured as graphs, such as road networks and traffic prediction.

My research interests include optimization of graph neural networks, improving the explainibility of graph machine learning, and utilization of graph-based models as an enhancement for large language models. I have done study in devising machine learning architectures mores suited for graph data that is constantly evolving over time, known in the field as continuou time dynamic graphs (CTDGs). Currently, the focus of my research seeks to improve graph models' abilities to incorporate the information of nodes that are far away from one another and that which are relevant to the intended output of the model.
